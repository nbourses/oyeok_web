January 17, 2017

With the demonetisation move the country's quick flush of black money would ensure a violent drop in real estate prices. This gave a lot of potential investors hope that they could purchase bigger houses for reasonably lower prices. But can we really invest so readily in real estate now? Market research shows a relatively low level of interest in real estate for the sole reason that the prices for real estate in big metropolitans are relatively higher than the average income of the citizens and therefore the citizens see no reason why they should invest in properties because they are far too expensive. But the use of property portals like OyeOk enables consumers to look at the average minimum and average maximum rates for real estate in specific parts and regions of the cities they wish to invest in. The app will help citizens judge whether they really wish to invest or not in property right now by connecting them to brokers who can brief them on the current status of the market. Real estate prices are bound to rise again as realtors will definitely need to have a profit margin and with the current situation many people may favour the price drop and invest in properties and also experts say that is the ripe time to invest in properties because now realtors are short in cash and the prices are more realistic and also to only invest in ready to move in properties as now that realtors are low on cash any upcoming projects will ask for payment and their lack of cash may result in failure of the project or slow progress which could mean investors will have their revenue tied up in projects that may never be completed or will take too long.So ultimately maybe you can say #OkJaanu to Real Estate now but it's upto your approach.
