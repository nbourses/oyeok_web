January 17, 2017

When investing in Real Estate you have to pick wisely to gain maximum profit from the properties you invest in. To identify these properties you will have to do a good amount of research. You can do all the research yourself or you can hire a broker who knows the market very well. It is advisable to hire a broker and then if you have a considerable amount of revenue with you, by striking good deal you can buy a property with enough tactical planning for eg; if you live in a city like Bangalore or Pune where there are good colleges for Under Graduate studies you can buy a property next to these so that even if you charge a rent that is a little higher than the average you will attracting tenants that are more willing to pay for the rent to decrease their travel time. Or if you find a good deal on a flat near industrial areas in cities like Mumbai or Delhi then you can attract tenants who are willing to pay higher rent charges to stay closer to their offices therefore you will have a profitable inflow of revenue every month. You also have to be flexible in the Indian market because it is common for landlords in India to prevent opposite gender tenants to live together and a lot of tenants will be of those kind and often turning them down can stereotype you as a "Khadoos" landlord and the chances of acquiring a tenant will gradually decrease. Also invest in properties that are in your price range, if you see a property that is outstanding and you have just the amount of revenue needed to purchase it but it's profitability is low, DO NOT PURCHASE IT. Why? Because you will be paying a lot more than you can earn from that property. Research is of the utmost importance. Do you want to furnish your properties or not? Well furnished flats are far more valuable and it is a good opinion to include all the basic amenities needed and this should satisfy the tenants and also allow you to marginally increase the rent. And that is how to get #Raees when investing in Indian Real Estate.
