January 17, 2017

Can't get change? Found yourself unable to pay the rickshaw because you only have 2000 rupee notes? Well we understand. On 8th November 2016 our PM Narendra Modi announced the demonetisation move and that definitely demoralised most of us. Why? We would all have to stand in long lines outside banks in the scorching Mumbai heat to exchange our now void old 500 and 1000 rupee notes. The move caused havoc amongst the citizens of India and then the office going had regularly found themselves standing in these lines desperately awaiting their turn to exchange their old notes. Other also standing in line to deposit the cash into their bank accounts. Overnight E-Wallets had become the catalyst for regular payment for most of the people in the city. How is the situation currently being dealt with though? As I mentioned above E-Wallets have become one of the more prominent methods of payment, lots of Auto Rickshaws have and those stuck with 2000 rupee notes are often perplexed as to how to acquire some change for the money. Other ways of wrestling the current situation is heavy reliance on your debit cards. This is often very tedious as certain services will not allow you to pay by your card and you will need hard cash and for that you will most certainly have to stand in the excessively long lines. Now that is unfavourable. You want our opinion? Use any E-Wallet application. You will benefit highly from those.
